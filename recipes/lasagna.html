Lasagna

A classic Italian dish!

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made with layers of pasta, meat, cheese, and tomato sauce. It is a hearty and comforting meal that is perfect for feeding a crowd. Lasagna can be made with a variety of fillings, including beef, sausage, chicken, or vegetables. It is typically topped with mozzarella cheese and baked until bubbly and golden brown.

The origins of lasagna can be traced back to ancient Greece, where flat pasta sheets were layered with cheese and baked. The dish was later adopted by the Romans, who added meat and tomato sauce to the recipe. Today, lasagna is enjoyed all over the world and is a staple of Italian cuisine.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 can of tomato sauce
  • 1 can of crushed tomatoes
  • 2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 1 teaspoon of dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon of dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 16 ounces of ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 3 cups of shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup of grated parmesan cheese

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef, onion, and garlic over medium heat until the meat is browned and the vegetables are tender.
  2. Add the tomato sauce, crushed tomatoes, sugar, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper to the skillet. Simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Meanwhile, cook the lasagna noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese and egg. Mix well.
  5. Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. Spread a thin layer of the meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  7. Arrange 4 lasagna noodles over the sauce. Top with a third of the ricotta mixture, a third of the mozzarella cheese, and a third of the parmesan cheese.
  8. Repeat the layers two more times, ending with the remaining meat sauce and cheeses.
  9. Cover the baking dish with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
  10. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the lasagna is bubbly and golden brown.
